## 🎨 Photo Selection Cheat Sheet

### For Each Photo, Ask:

#### ✅ YES - Use This Photo If:
- Sharp focus (not blurry)
- Good natural lighting
- Clean, no clutter visible
- Horizontal orientation (landscape)
- Makes you feel "I want to go there"
- Shows hotel at its best
- Professional appearance

#### ❌ NO - Skip This Photo If:
- Blurry or out of focus
- Too dark or heavy shadows
- Messy backgrounds
- Vertical orientation (portrait)
- People's faces visible
- Flash glare or reflections
- Feels dated or unprofessional

## 💡 Pro Tips for Non-Photographers

### Room Photos:
- Stand in corner, shoot diagonally
- Open all curtains for natural light
- Make sure bed is perfectly made
- Remove personal items, clutter

### Food Photos:
- Shoot from above (bird's eye view)
- Use natural light from window
- Clean, white plates work best
- Show one dish, not whole table

### Exterior Photos:
- Golden hour = 1 hour before sunset
- Avoid cars, trash in frame
- Show entrance clearly
- Include some flowers/plants for warmth

### Quick Quality Check:
1. View photo at 100% zoom - Is it sharp?
2. Stand 6 feet from screen - Does it look inviting?
3. Show to a friend - Do they say "wow"?

If yes to all three = Use it!

## 🆘 Troubleshooting

**Q: Photo is too dark**
A: Take new photo near window during daytime, or increase brightness in editing

**Q: Photo is blurry**
A: Hold phone/camera steady, use burst mode and pick sharpest one

**Q: Can't decide between two photos**
A: Show both to 3 people, ask "which makes you want to visit?" - use winner

**Q: Photo file is still too large after optimization**
A: Reduce quality setting by 5-10%, or reduce dimensions by 10%
